BA/BSc (Honours) Open degree: Bachelor of Arts (Honours) Open route

Bachelor of Arts (Honours) Open (QD BA H 1)

60 credits transferred, 300 credits left to complete

If you choose to register on this route you would need to complete all the remaining credit within 14 years.

Shown below are the groups that appear in this route and the amount of credit that you could transfer to each group. Click on the group title for more details of the modules you would need to complete if you chose this route.

There are two types of credit transfer. Where you see a module marked with "Module Credit Applied" we have determined that your previous study was a very close match to this particular module. You will not need to study this module, even if it was listed as a compulsory module for this qualification.

Where you see modules marked as "Group Credit Applied" you do not need to study the module in this group but if the module appears in another group as well you will be able to study it.

Sometimes we have only been able to transfer some of the credit required from a group, say 30 credits in a group that requires you to complete 60 credits. If this is the case you will need to complete OU modules to bring your total credits from that group to the required number. You will be able to gain this credit by studying one of the unmarked modules shown in the group.

We have awarded 60 credits with equal BA/BSc designation.

Our Open degree with honours can be awarded as either a Bachelor of Arts or Bachelor of Science honours degree.

To qualify for a BA (Hons) Open degree you must have at least 180 credits from modules or credit transfer suitable for a BA. If your credit transfer has been awarded with a BA designation or with an equal BA/BSc designation then it can count towards the 180 credits that must be suitable for a BA. Of this 180 credits, at least 60 credits must be at level 2 and 60 credits at level 3. If your credit transfer is awarded at level 2 it can be used in place of this requirement but you will need to complete 60 credits at level 3 of OU study with the appropriate designation. You can use credit transfer that has been awarded with a BSc designation towards a BA (Hons) if you also include within your degree 180 credits of modules suitable for a BA and at the required levels.

OU modules suitable for a BA degree begin with A, B, D, E, K, L, M, U or W.

You'll start with 60 credits of Level 1 modules, choosing from a wide and varied list of key introductory modules (worth 30 or 60 credits each). You can see the full list when you register, but here we suggest some of the modules that introduce our 14 subjects: Read more...

  • Arts and humanities - The arts past and present (AA100) (60 credits)
