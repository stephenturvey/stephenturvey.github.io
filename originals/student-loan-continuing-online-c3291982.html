Your student loan for BSc (Honours) Criminology and Psychological Studies

To complete your loan application and enrolment, follow these steps:

  • 1 Take note of our guidance
  • 2 Apply for your loan
  • 3 Complete the fee loan process

1Step 1 Take note of our guidance

Completing an online loan application with is straightforward but to ensure you complete it correctly you should note down the answers to the following questions before beginning your application. It is important that you use the information below to avoid a delay in the approval of your loan application.

SFE Question Answer
What course will you be studying?

You will only need to answer this question if you have changed your course.

For your BSc (Honours) Criminology and Psychological Studies you need to enter Named Honours (Sciences).

On the online application form, start to type the qualification name as shown above in the box provided. Select the qualification that exactly matches and is followed by the month in which your qualification starts in this academic year.

For example if you're studying Named Honours (Arts) and your first module in this academic year starts in October, select 'Named Honours (Arts) (October)'.
In this academic year when will you start your course?

This is the start date of your first module in this seasonal academic year. Seasonal academic years are different to the OU's academic year. The table below will help you identify when your seasonal academic year starts.

Your course start is the start date of the first module in your seasonal academic year.

How long is your course? This will be the number of years in which you currently expect to complete your qualification with the OU. Student Finance England is expecting an estimated date at this stage; it does not matter if your plans change.
How much is the tuition fee you will be charged by the Open University?

This will depend on the number of credits you intend to study in this seasonal academic year. Insert the total of the fee(s) from the modules you are planning to study in your seasonal academic year. Use the information on Seasonal Academic Year to find out when your seasonal academic year starts and include all module fees you intend to study in the 12 months from that date.

For example:

30 credits 60 credits 90 credits 120 credits
£1316 £2632 £3948 £5264

The fee for the Professional Graduate Certificate in Education "PGCE" is £6750 in year 1 and £2250 in year two.
How much would you like to borrow? Click on the option "Maximum I'm entitled to". This means that you'll only have to make one application to Student Finance England for your intended study for this academic year. By selecting this option, the full amount of the fee you have stated in the question above can be paid for with your Tuition Fee Loan. The OU will only be paid the amount actually needed to cover your module fees and you will only be liable for the amount of loan that you actually use. If the OU amends your Tuition Fee Loan with Student Finance England you will receive notification from the OU and Student Finance England to confirm your change of circumstance.

2Step 2 Apply for your loan

Complete your online application

3Step 3 Completing the fee loan process

After Student Finance England has processed your loan application, they'll send you a Student Finance Entitlement Letter. This confirms your loan has been successful. At the same time, they will enclose a Student Finance Declaration Form which you must sign and return as soon as possible. If you do not return the Student Finance Declaration Form your loan application will not be finalised and SFE will not pay your tuition fees and we will ask you to pay your fees using an alternative payment method.

Once you have returned the declaration SFE will pay your tuition fees directly to the Open University a minimum of 14 days after the start of your first module.
