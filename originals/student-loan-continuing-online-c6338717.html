Your student loan for BA (Honours) Business Studies

To complete your enrolment, follow these steps:

  • 1 Take note of our guidance
  • 2 Apply for your loan
  • 3 Supply CRN
  • 4 Complete the fee loan process

Attention: If you've already applied for your Tuition Fee Loan, skip straight to Step 2.


1Step 1 Take note of our guidance

You'll need to apply online to Student Finance England (SFE). Completing an online loan application with SFE is straightforward but to ensure you complete it correctly you should note down the answers to the following questions before beginning your application. It is important that you use the information below to avoid any delay in the approval of your loan application.

The online application will take you about 30 minutes.

To apply, you'll need:

  • the answers to the questions in the table below
  • for UK applicants applying for the first time, details from your current, valid UK passport (you can apply with another form of identity evidence, but using your passport is quicker and easier)
  • your National Insurance number (if you haven't already given it to SFE)
SFE Question Answer
What course will you be studying?

For your BA (Honours) Business Studies you need to enter Named Honours (Arts).

On the online application form, start to type the qualification name as shown above in the box provided. Select the qualification that exactly matches and is followed by the month in which your qualification starts in this academic year.

For example if you're studying Named Honours (Arts) and your first module in this academic year starts in October, select 'Named Honours (Arts) (October)'.
In this academic year when will you start your course?

This is the start date of your first module in this academic year.

Note: If you've studied previously, but this is your first loan application you will need to apply for your loan for the correct seasonal academic year. For further information see Seasonal Academic Year.
How long is your course? This will be the number of years in which you currently expect to complete your qualification with the OU. Student Finance England is expecting an estimated date at this stage; it does not matter if your plans change.
How much is the tuition fee you will be charged by the Open University?

This will depend on the number of credits you intend to study in your academic year. Insert the total of the fee(s) from the modules you intend to study in your 2014/15 academic year.

For example:

30 credits 60 credits 90 credits 120 credits
£1316 £2632 £3948 £5264

The fee for the Professional Graduate Certificate in Education "PGCE" is £6750 in year 1 and £2250 in year two.

Note: If you've studied previously, but this is your first loan application you will need to apply for your loan for module fees in the correct academic year. For further information see Seasonal Academic Year.
How much would you like to borrow?

Click on the option "Maximum I'm entitled to". This means that you'll only have to make one application to Student Finance England for your intended study for this academic year. By selecting this option, the full amount of the fee you have stated in the question above can be paid for with your Tuition Fee Loan.

The OU will only be paid the amount actually needed to cover your module fees and you will only be liable for the amount of loan that you actually use. If the OU amends your Tuition Fee Loan with Student Finance England you will receive notification from the OU and Student Finance England to confirm your change of circumstance.

2Step 2 Apply for your loan

Complete your online application

3Step 3 Use your CRN to complete your enrolment

If this is your first Tuition Fee Loan application you will be given a CRN (Customer Reference Number) when you create an account with SFE using their online portal. If you have already used a Tuition Fee Loan to pay for tuition fees at another institution, you can use your existing CRN to complete your enrolment.

Once you have completed and submitted your Tuition Fee Loan application to SFE you're ready to complete your enrolment. To do this we'll ask you for your CRN number as proof that you've registered and applied for your Tuition Fee Loan, so make sure you have this to hand before you proceed.

By telling us your Customer Reference Number (CRN) and completing your enrolment you are accepting our Conditions of Registration and Fee Rules. Failure to fully complete the fee loan application process after telling us your CRN will mean you continue to be liable for all your tuition fees and we will ask you to pay your fees by an alternative payment method. See Step 4 to ensure you fully complete the fee loan application process.

Complete your enrolment by returning your registration documents to us in the envelope provided. Please ensure you have completed the registration documents and included your CRN number.

You'll also be able to complete enrolment on any other modules that you've asked us to hold for you. We'll remind you to do this from StudentHome.

When you've completed your enrolment on DB123 You and your money: personal finance in context, we'll begin to send you the information you need to start studying.

Note: your records will update overnight, after which we'll display your CRN on this page.

4Step 4 Completing the fee loan process

After SFE have processed your fee loan application, they'll send you a Student Finance Entitlement pack. This confirms that your loan has been successful. At the same time, they will enclose a Student Finance Declaration Form which you must sign and return as soon as possible. If you do not return the Student Finance Declaration Form to SFE your loan application will not be finalised and SFE will not cover your tuition fees.

Once you have returned the Student Finance Declaration Form, SFE will pay your tuition fees directly to the Open University a minimum of 14 days after the start of your first module.
