BA (Honours) English Literature : Creative writing pathway

This pathway is made up of 360 credits. 60 of your credits could be eligible to count towards this.

Shown below are the groups of modules that appear in this pathway and the amount of your existing credit that could count in each group. Click on the group title for more details of the modules you need to complete if you choose this pathway.

Where a module is marked with "Enrolled" or "Completed", our records show that you are currently studying, or have successfully completed the module. If you have completed a module, you cannot study it again.

If you want to request this change, use the green button to have this change checked by one of our advisers. We will confirm by email which of your credits, if any, can count in line with the qualification rules. You will then be asked to confirm that you want to register on this qualification. This qualification, and the module choices available to you, will be confirmed in StudentHome.

Our curriculum and regulations change each year so there may be a version of this qualification which may be a better option for you. If this is the case we will contact you to discuss it.

You need to complete all the remaining credit within 16 years of the first module that is counting towards the qualification. For example, if your first module counting towards the qualification began in 2010, this is taken as the start of your qualification and you would need to complete the qualification by 2026.

It is important that you check the student, module and specific qualification regulations each year to understand how they apply to you and how they may have changed.

Alternatively, you can speak with your Student Support Team who can discuss your options. Their contact details are available from the 'Contact us' button in the StudentHome Help Centre.

Stage 1

You'll begin your studies with the compulsory 60-credit module The arts past and present (AA100), an absorbing and broad-based grounding in the arts and humanities, including: Read more...

  • history
  • philosophy
  • music
  • literature
  • art history
  • classical studies
  • religious studies.

You'll develop essential study skills while engaging with a wide range of topics, periods and approaches and enhancing your enjoyment and understanding of fascinating and diverse aspects of human culture.

We recommend that you then build on this with Voices, texts and material culture (A105). This module will extend your understanding of the humanities, with creative writing, English language and heritage studies added to the rich mix of subjects you will have encountered in AA100. It is designed to extend the range of your interdisciplinary perceptions, hone your critical and analytical skills, provide you with opportunities to work collaboratively, develop flexibility in your writing skills and prepare you for study at Stage 2.

Alternatively, you could choose from other options which include languages, social sciences and design.

Stage 2

Stage 2 begins with the core 60-credit module Reading and studying literature (A230) which is a varied and wide-ranging introduction to the study of literature, designed to equip you with different approaches and techniques for reading texts. You'll explore: Read more...

  • a selection of texts from the Renaissance to the present day
  • a stimulating mix of classic texts and less well-known works
  • a range of genres, including drama, poetry and prose fiction as well as autobiography, travel-writing and film
  • how contemporary texts make use of literature of the past.

Then the 60-credit module Creative writing (A215) will let you focus on developing your creative writing skills. Through practical exercises, activities and readings, all designed to sustain your writing, you'll explore:

  • the creative process; ways of using your memory and experience and how to develop a daily discipline
  • approaches to writing fiction
  • approaches to writing poetry
  • approaches to life writing - biography and autobiography
  • publishing standards and how to revise and present your work to a professional standard.

Throughout your Stage 2 studies you'll continue to develop your critical thinking, writing and analysis skills. You'll also begin to work and think more independently in relation to a range of problems and questions posed by your course of study.

Stage 3

At Stage 3 you'll study a 60-credit English Literature module, with current options including: Read more...

  • Shakespeare: text and performance (AA306): covers a range of Shakespeare's dramatic plays and poetic work
  • The nineteenth-century novel (AA316): focuses on understanding the novel's relationship with society and how it developed through the works of Charles Dickens and Jane Austen, amongst others
  • 20th century literature: texts and debates (A300): featuring key writers such as Chekhov and Virginia Woolf.
English literature from Shakespeare to Austen (A334) is planned for October 2015.

You'll also study Advanced creative writing (A363) (60 credits), which gives you the opportunity to further develop your creative writing skills, widening your generic range and knowledge of style. You'll explore:

  • form and genre; how to sustain longer and more complex works of fiction and poetry
  • writing for stage, screen and radio
  • professional layouts for dramatic media
  • how scriptwriting skills might enhance and influence your fiction, poetry and life writing
  • how to improve your individual voice and style, no matter the genre.

You can take these modules in any order. You'll work more independently than you did at Levels 1 and 2, and devote some of your study time to exploring topics and issues in greater detail.
