Disability Information
Dyslexia, dyspraxia and dyscalcula.
General and Educational Information
Button has informed us that she has dyslexia, dyscalculia and dyspraxia (assessed in 2010). Her study may be affected in the following ways:

- Reading

Button has told us that she is able to read for an hour if she has a focus, i.e. she is reading to learn.

Tutors are asked to break down materials into short chunks to keep concentration levels high and to help with assimilation of material.

Button can struggle to read a computer screen for a long period of time. She explained that she is slightly colour-blind and prefers to read black ink on a yellow background or black ink ona pink background if the subject-matter is more complex or uses longer words.

-Writing Assignments

Button struggles to concentrate for long periods of time. Spelling longer words can cause problems, especially if they sound similar. Button feels that her grammar has improved a lot, but she does find numbers challenging, particularly when she is tired. Typing for long periods can be difficult as Button loses concentration and can get frustrated.

Tutors are asked to ensure she understands the requirements and give help with the best strategies for planning and structuring work. Tu tors are also asked to refer to the marking guidelines for students with dyslexia.

- Note-taking and tutorials

Button generally finds note-taking ok because she is focused on the task in hand. However, tutors are asked to provide summary notes of tutors when available and circulate any handouts in advance.

- Exams

Clear guidance on revision for any examinations is requested.
